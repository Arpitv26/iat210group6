**Dynamics/Tradeoff/Dilemmas**

A successful strategy we found was to immediately try and invade somebody’s castle during the first stages of the game. Most players were too preoccupied with racing towards the cheese closest to them. So if one player immediately went for somebody else’s castle, there was usually nobody near by to intercept them. This would knock down the opponents pre-existing cheese quantity while increasing your own. The usage of the Carrier Pigeon and Necromicer card was also a very common strategy for players due to its effectiveness

Players have to choose whether they want to risk going over a hazard tile, as there is a chance they will have to draw from the hazard deck. Also, tunnels serve as a great strategic way to move around the board, but the chance of getting a hazard gave some players a dilemma on whether they wanted to risk the chance getting a hazard on them 

Play sessions usually lasted around 1 hour for players with prior experience. New players took a bit longer as they needed to understand each unit’s ability, how combat worked, how hazard and tunnel tiles are used, and overall comprehension of the board
